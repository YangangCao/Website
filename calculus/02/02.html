Limits and Derivatives

Problems Lead to Limits and Derivatives

  • Tangent line: use secant line generated by \(2\) points getting closer to estimate tangent line

  • Instantaneous velocity: use average velocity in a shorter period time to estimate instantaneous velocity

Limit of a Function

Definition of Limit

Limit of a function is written as

\[ \lim _{x \rightarrow a} f(x) = L \]

it means that the limit of \(f\) as \(x\) approaches \(a\) equals \(L\).

Definition of the Term Approache

  • Informally, approache means getting closer to a certain value

  • Formally, as \(x\) approaches \(a\) means: \(f(x)\) is defined on \((a-\delta, a+\delta)\) or \((a-\delta, a) \cup (a,a+\delta)\), where \(\delta\) is an abitary small number, and \(x\) take value in any possible way such that \(|x-a| < D\), where \(D\) is another arbitary small number, and limit will exist if \(f(x)-L < R\) where \(R\) is any possible arbitary small number, whenever \(|x-a| < D\)

One-Sided Limits

For left-hand limit:

\[ \lim _{x \rightarrow a^{-}} f(x) = L \]

For right-hand limit:

\[ \lim _{x \rightarrow a^{+}} f(x) = L \]

Note that

\[ \lim _{x \rightarrow a} f(x) = L \]

exists only when both left-hand and right-hand side limits exist.

Infinite Limits

Let \(f(x)\) is defined on \((a-\delta, a) \cup (a,a+\delta)\)

\[ \lim _{x \rightarrow a} f(x) = \infty \]

leads to infinite limits.

  • here \(\infty\) can be either \(+\infty\) or \(-\infty\)

  • it often occurs when the function is not defined at \(a\) or \(a\) is a pole of ration functions
